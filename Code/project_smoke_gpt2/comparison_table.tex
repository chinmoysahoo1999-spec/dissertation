\noindent\textbf{Backbone:} GPT-2 (124M parameters). \textbf{Training set:} 800 MIND-generated Wikipedia records (500 per class, seed=42). \textbf{Test split:} 200 records, identical seed across all methods $\Rightarrow$ direct AUROC comparison. \textbf{Cells:} AUROC; green-shaded \textbf{bold} = column winner.

\vspace{0.3cm}

\noindent\textbf{Methodology (baselines, paper-faithful):}
\begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{-2pt}
  \item \textbf{SAPLMA} (Azaria \& Mitchell 2023): MLP probe $768{\to}256{\to}128{\to}64{\to}1$ on layer-7 last-token hidden. Supervised.
  \item \textbf{HaloScope} (Du et al., NeurIPS 2024): spectral score $\to$ percentile pseudo-labels $\to$ non-linear probe $768{\to}1024{\to}1$. Unsup. pseudo-labels.
  \item \textbf{HalluShift} (Dasgupta, IJCNN 2025): 31-d Wasserstein/cosine on hidden+attention + 11 token-prob features $\to$ \texttt{CombinedNN}. Supervised.
  \item \textbf{EigenScore / INSIDE} (Chen et al., ICLR 2024): K=10 sampled responses, K$\times$K covariance, log-det score. Unsupervised. Downstream subsampled to n=100, K=3.
\end{itemize}

\noindent\textbf{Notes on interpretation.} GPT-2 (124M) is a deliberately small backbone for this smoke run; signal at this scale is noisy. Headline observations:
\begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{-2pt}
  \item \textbf{HalluShift} wins the average multi-task AUROC (0.524) and 4 of 7 downstream columns.
  \item Our variants dominate \textbf{Wikipedia held-out} (in-distribution) and pick up TruthfulQA + TriviaQA.
  \item All methods collapse on HaluEval-QA at GPT-2 scale (domain shift; supervised MLPs trained on MIND-Wikipedia are anti-correlated with the right answer).
  \item For scale-up: variant C (+V\_last alone) is the only variant that holds its own against the baselines on transfer (avg-MT 0.490) and is worth re-testing on Qwen-3B.
\end{itemize}
